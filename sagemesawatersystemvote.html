Synopsis of Sage Mesa Water Acquisition

Introduction

  • RDOS plans to charge approx. $12,000/year per household for potable water if the referendum passes.
  • Long-term cost could exceed $250,000 per household.
  • This may reduce property values and lead to mortgage defaults.
  • The water utility is not owned by residents and should not be their responsibility.
  • The Province has managed the system for 35 years, subcontracting to RDOS.

The synopsis outlines issues around mismanagement, financial risks, and government responsibility, while suggesting community action. Residents are encouraged to review the McElhanney Assessment Report and RDOS meeting videos for more context.

Estimated Annual Household Water Cost

  • Debt Service: $8,800–$9,400
  • Operations & Maintenance: $1,800–$2,500
  • Water Fee: $1,132
  • Total: Approx. $12,382/year

Summary Points

  • The Province assumed responsibility in 1990 and failed to collect adequate reserve funds.
  • The system was not maintained properly, leading to infrastructure risk.
  • Acquiring the system means inheriting a $33M liability.
  • A yes vote transfers responsibility away from the Province and RDOS.
  • The McElhanney report recommends connecting to Penticton’s system as most cost-effective.
  • The community must apply political pressure to secure provincial funding support.
